Scope of Work

What's Included

Complete breakdown by trade. This is a shell/rough package — all finish materials and fixtures are customer-supplied. Timberline provides all labor, structural framing, and installation.

1

Framing & Drywall

New Dividing Wall · Reframe Openings · Blocking · Drywall · Insulation
  • New dividing wall between the two bathrooms — interior, non-load-bearing framing
  • Reframe and close the old shared Jack-and-Jill openings
  • Frame two new separate entrance openings (one per bath)
  • Shower wall blocking for both showers — solid backing for glass, grab bars, and fixtures
  • Full drywall throughout both bathrooms — all new walls finished and ready for paint
  • Interior sound insulation between the two bathrooms
2

Plumbing

Shower Drains · Slab Trench · Vanities · W/D Rough · Toilets

One consolidated plumbing scope covering all rough and finish plumbing for both bathrooms and the washer/dryer area:

  • Relocate both shower drains to new positions — includes slab cutting and trenching
  • Move supply and waste lines for both single-sink vanities
  • Washer/dryer rough-in — supply, drain, and venting to the new W/D wall
  • Set both toilets (toilets customer-supplied)
  • Final hookup for both vanities once cabinets and tops are set
3

Showers — Two Custom Builds

Hot Mop Pans · Tile Labor · Semi-Frameless Glass
  • Two custom hot-mop mud-set shower pans (36×34 and 56×34) — the critical waterproofing layer
  • Full tile-setting labor for both showers — walls and floors (tile material customer-supplied)
  • Two semi-frameless glass enclosures — measured, fabricated, and installed

All tile, trim, grout, shower valves, trim kits, and shower heads are customer-supplied. Timberline installs.

4

Electrical

240V Dryer · GFCI Outlets · Lighting · Vent Fans
  • Relocate 240V circuit to the new W/D wall for the dryer
  • GFCI outlets in both bathrooms — code-compliant placement
  • Vanity and bath lighting wiring — customer supplies fixtures, Timberline wires and sets
  • Two vent fans — tied into existing roof penetrations (no new roof work needed)
5

Doors & Hardware

2 New Entrance Doors · Supply & Hang
  • Two new hollow-core 6-panel entrance doors — one per bathroom, supplied and hung
  • Lockset hardware is customer-provided
6

Paint & Finish

Both Baths · Walls · Trim · Complete
  • Full paint in both bathrooms — walls, ceiling, and all trim
  • Clean, professional finish ready for fixtures and accessories
7

Trim, Millwork & Vanity Set

Baseboard · Door Casing · Vanity Cabinets & Tops
  • Baseboard — material and labor throughout both bathrooms
  • Door casing — material and labor for both new entrance doors
  • Set both vanity cabinets and countertops (cabinets and tops customer-supplied; plumber handles final hookup under the plumbing scope)
8

Architectural Drawings

Architectural · Plumbing · Electrical · Manor Alt. Submittal
  • Complete architectural, plumbing, and electrical drawings prepared for the Manor Alterations submittal
  • Required by United's process — these need to be drawn before any work can begin

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we hear most on projects like this.

What does "shell/rough package" mean?
We handle all the structural work, rough-in trades (plumbing, electrical, framing), and installation labor. You purchase every finish material — tile, fixtures, vanities, toilets, etc. — giving you full control over selections and material costs. There's no markup on anything you buy yourself. When your materials arrive, we install them.
Why is plumbing one consolidated number?
Because the plumbing scope touches so many areas (two shower drains with slab trenching, two vanity supply/waste relocations, W/D rough-in, and setting two toilets), we price it as one all-in number to avoid double-counting across sub-trades. Everything plumbing-related is in that one line — no hidden extras.
What about the waste-line inspection?
Per Standard 43 §3.3, the Mutual plumbing waste-line inspection and clearance must be completed before any demo begins. This is your responsibility as the member. We strongly recommend scheduling this early — if it isn't pulled first, you could be responsible for any resulting plumbing repairs downstream.
Who handles demo and abatement?
Demo, abatement, air clearance, and dumpster are outside Timberline's scope. You'll contract that separately (typically PW Stephens or a similar abatement company). Once demo is complete and air clearance is confirmed, we pick up from there.
What are the Manor Alteration drawings?
United requires architectural, plumbing, and electrical drawings as part of their Manor Alterations submittal process. These need to be drawn and approved before construction can begin. The $3,500 covers having those drawings prepared and ready for submittal.
Is any structural engineering needed?
All framing in this project is interior and non-load-bearing — the new dividing wall, the reframed openings, and the shower blocking. That means no structural engineering is triggered. If something unexpected comes up during construction that requires engineering, that would be the owner's responsibility and priced separately.
What's your warranty on the work?
All labor is warrantied for 1 year from project completion. Materials and fixtures carry their manufacturer warranties on top of that.
