Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions homeowners ask us most before signing.

Why do I supply my own tile, fixtures, and appliances?
This keeps your costs transparent and gives you full control over what goes into your home. You pick exactly what you want, pay the exact retail price, and we handle all the installation. There's no markup on materials you purchase yourself — our price covers the labor to install them.
What happens if you find something unexpected during demo?
On a whole-house project, it's possible we'll uncover something not visible before demo — water damage, outdated wiring, subfloor issues. If that happens, we stop, show you what we found, and price the fix before any additional work happens. You approve it in writing first. Nothing gets added to your bill without your sign-off.
How does scheduling work across all four scopes?
We sequence the work to minimize disruption and maximize efficiency. Typically: demo and rough work (electrical, plumbing, framing) happen first across all areas, then we move room by room through finishes. You won't have every room torn apart at once — we plan it so you can still live in the home during most of the process.
What about the kitchen countertop estimate?
The countertop line is estimated based on standard quartz with a 4-inch backsplash. The final cost depends on the specific material you select — premium slabs, exotic colors, or waterfall edges would adjust the price. Once you've picked a material, we'll confirm the final number before ordering.
What about the master bath countertop?
The fabrication labor (cutting, templating, and installing) is included in the master bath price. The countertop material itself is not factored in yet — once you select a material, we'll confirm the cost and add it. Guest bath is the same: fabrication labor included, material supplied by you.
What do the permit fees cover?
The $4,000 permit estimate covers building, electrical, and plumbing permit applications with the City of San Juan Capistrano. Actual fees are set by the city and may vary slightly. The 5% coordination fee covers our time managing the permit process — filing applications, scheduling required inspections at each phase, meeting with inspectors on site, and making sure everything passes. This keeps the project moving without you having to deal with the city directly.
What's your warranty on the work?
All labor is warrantied for 1 year from project completion. Materials and fixtures carry their manufacturer warranties on top of that.
