01 — Project Overview
A Complete Guest Bath Transformation

This project is a full remodel of the guest bathroom at 10 Calle Pastadero — demo the existing tub surround, flooring, and vanity, open up the wall between the vanity and wet room, and come back with a tiled shower over a new alcove tub, a double-sink vanity with quartz top, and all new plumbing, electrical, and finishes throughout.

All work is performed by Timberline Build Co and our trusted trade partners, with Justin Sherman on-site throughout the project.

This is a construction-labor proposal. Timberline handles all demo, framing, rough-in, tile installation, glass, electrical, plumbing, paint, and finish work. You select and purchase all finish materials (tile, fixtures, vanity, tub) — we install everything. Your estimated material budget is broken out separately in Section 05 so you know exactly what to plan for.

02 — Scope of Work
What's Included
  • Full demo — tub, surround, flooring, and vanity. Existing toilet stays (protected in place). Remove soffit over vanity area
  • Dumpster and haul-away for all demo debris
  • Partial wall removal between vanity area and wet room to open up the space — assumes non-bearing (see note below)
  • Framing — blocking for shower niche and glass mount
  • Drywall — patch ceiling and walls at demo areas, soffit area, wall behind mirror, texture match throughout
  • Tile labor — shower walls (32×60 tub surround, ~105 sf including surround area) and bathroom floor (~45 sf). All tile material customer-supplied
  • Semi-frameless shower glass enclosure — measured, fabricated, and installed
  • Plumbing — all-in: shower valve rough and finish, set new alcove tub ($300 tub allowance included — if your selection exceeds $300, the difference is billed as a change order), double vanity rough and finish (supply, drain, and faucet hookup), reset existing toilet
  • Countertop fabrication — quartz fabrication labor for double-sink top (×2 sink cutouts). Customer supplies slab material
  • Set vanity cabinet and countertop (cabinet customer-supplied; plumber handles final hookup)
  • Electrical — relocate vanity lighting and outlets from removed wall, add 3 recessed can lights (6"), GFCI outlet in bathroom (code), vent fan (new or relocated, tied to existing penetration)
  • Paint — entire bathroom: walls, ceiling, and trim
  • Baseboard — labor and material throughout bathroom
⚠️ Note: The wall between the vanity and wet room is assumed to be non-bearing. This will be confirmed before any work begins. If it turns out to be load-bearing, structural engineering and a beam/post solution would need to be added — estimated at $1,500–$2,500 additional.
03 — Exclusions
What's NOT Included
  • All finish materials — tile, vanity cabinet, countertop slab, sinks, faucets, shower fixtures, and light fixtures are customer-supplied (see estimated material budget in Section 05)
  • Permits — not included. Customer is responsible for permits if desired
  • Toilet accessories and hardware — customer to supply
  • Countertop slab material — fabrication labor is included, but stone cost depends on your slab selection (budget $45–90/sf for a ~7' double top)
  • Structural engineering — only required if the wall is determined to be load-bearing
  • Toilet flange height adjustment — existing toilet is being reused; if the new floor tile buildup requires a flange adjustment, that would be priced separately
  • Any work not explicitly listed in this proposal
