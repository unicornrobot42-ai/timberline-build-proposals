Plans, Architecture & Permitting

Start immediately · Foundation for everything
✅ Start Now

Before any construction begins, we need a full set of architectural drawings — both as-is (the home as it exists today) and projected (as we intend to build it). These are produced by a licensed architect and structural engineer and become the legal backbone of the entire project.

Once drawings are complete, we submit to the city for permit approval. Timberline manages the entire submittal process — fielding city comments, making required revisions, and driving the plans to a final approved set.

Why This Comes First

Without approved plans, any budget is an estimate. With approved plans, we give you a fixed-price contract. Phase 0 is the single most important investment in making this project run smoothly and on budget.

Living Room Addition

Front of house · 6–8 ft extension

We'll extend the front of the home by 6 to 8 feet, adding meaningful living space. This is a full structural addition — new foundation, framing, roofing tie-in, exterior finish to match the existing home, windows, electrical, insulation, drywall, and interior paint.

Exact scope and cost will be finalized once architectural drawings define the footprint and structural requirements.

2

Kitchen Remodel + Whole-Home Flooring

Full kitchen redesign · 1,665 sq ft new floors

A complete kitchen transformation: wall removal to open up the layout, all-new cabinetry, countertops, backsplash, plumbing, and electrical. Appliances are not included in this scope (client to purchase separately, or we coordinate install only).

Simultaneously, all flooring throughout the entire home — approximately 1,665 square feet — is removed and replaced, including new baseboard and stair treads. New flooring ties every updated space together and sets the finish level for the whole house.

Upstairs Addition

2nd story expansion · Master bedroom + new bathroom

The most complex phase: a second-story addition that extends the master bedroom and adds a full bathroom. This requires structural engineering to ensure the first floor can support the new load, new framing, roofing tie-in, exterior finish, full bathroom build-out (tile, plumbing, fixtures, glass enclosure), electrical, drywall, insulation, flooring, and all interior finishes.

The range below reflects the significant variables in second-story addition work. Square footage added, bathroom finish level, and structural requirements are all determined by the plans — this number tightens most after Phase 0.
